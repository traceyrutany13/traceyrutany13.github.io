【本の内容】
The present volume contains a selection of papers that were read at the conference entitled Cognitive Approaches to English an international event organized to mark the 30th anniversary of English studies at the Faculty of Philosophy (Josip Juraj Strossmayer University Osijek) which was held in Osijek on October 18-19 2007. The participants were invited to discuss issues in cognitive accounts of English ranging from fundamental to methodological to interdisciplinary and applied ones. The volume is divided accordingly into four parts. Part 1 Motivation in grammar deals with various phenomena in the grammar of English in the broadest sense of the term all of which are shown to be motivated by phorical and/or metonymic operations. Part 2 Constructing meaning (between grammar and lexicon) carries five chapters dealing with concepts ranging from various peculiarities of form-meaning pairings (such as synonymy polysemy and figurative meanings) to concept formation. The four chapters that make up Part III are concerned with the phenomenon of interlinguistic and intercultural variation in the use of phorical and metonymic processes. The volume is rounded off by Part IV with its three papers attempting to reconsider some TEFL issues from a cognitive linguistic point of view.
